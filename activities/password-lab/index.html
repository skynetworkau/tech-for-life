How Brute Force Attacks Work
Brute force attacks try every possible combination of characters until they find the correct password. Modern computers can try billions of combinations per second. A simple 6-character password with only lowercase letters can be cracked in under a second. Adding length, uppercase, numbers, and symbols exponentially increases the time needed to crack a password.

Two-Factor Authentication (2FA) Demo

2FA adds a second layer of security beyond your password. Even if someone steals your password, they can't access your account without the second factor.

Why 2FA Matters
Two-Factor Authentication (also called Multi-Factor Authentication or MFA) requires two different types of verification:
  • Something you know (password)
  • Something you have (phone, security key)
  • Something you are (fingerprint, face)
Studies show 2FA blocks 99.9% of automated account attacks.

94% Password Reuse Rate
Studies show that 94% of people reuse passwords across multiple accounts. This is extremely dangerous! When one site gets breached, hackers immediately try those credentials on other popular sites (this is called "credential stuffing"). One compromised password can lead to multiple account takeovers.

How Hackers Attack Passwords

Dictionary Attack
Tries common words from dictionaries
Includes names, places, sports teams
Can crack simple passwords in seconds
Billions of attempts per second
Brute Force Attack
Tries every possible combination
Starts with short, simple passwords
Modern GPUs: billions of attempts/sec
Length is the best defence
