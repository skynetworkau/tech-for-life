Understanding GST

The $75,000 threshold you need to know

GST (Goods and Services Tax) is a 10% tax added to most goods and services. You must register for GST if your business turnover exceeds $75,000 per year.

$75k threshold

$0 $150,000
‚úÖ
GST Registration: Optional
Your projected turnover is under $75,000. You don't need to register for GST, but you can if you want to claim GST credits on business purchases.

ü§î Not registered for GST?

You cannot charge GST on your invoices. Keep it simple - just charge your rate without adding 10%.

üìä Registered for GST?

You must charge 10% GST and lodge BAS (Business Activity Statement) quarterly. You can claim GST back on business expenses.

‚è∞ When to register

Register when you hit $75k, or expect to in the next 12 months. Register online at ato.gov.au through your myGov account.

üí° Voluntary registration

Some people register early if they have lots of business expenses (to claim GST credits) or want to look more established.
