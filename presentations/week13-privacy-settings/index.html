Location Data Tells Stories

üìç Your location reveals more than you think

Even if you don't post photos, location metadata shows:

  • Where you live (every night 10pm-7am, same location)
  • Where you work or go to school (weekday patterns)
  • Your daily routine (coffee shop, gym, friend's house)
  • When you're home alone vs. away
  • Who you spend time with (overlapping locations)

This data is sold to advertisers, insurance companies, and data brokers. It never goes away.

  • Disable third-party cookies
  • Use DuckDuckGo for searches (doesn't track)

üç™ What Are Cookies?

Cookies = Small files websites store on your device

First-party cookies: Set by the website you're visiting. Usually necessary (login status, shopping cart, preferences).

Third-party cookies: Set by OTHER companies (ads, trackers). They follow you across websites to build a profile of your browsing habits.

What to do:

  • Block third-party cookies in browser settings
  • Clear cookies every few weeks
  • Don't click "Accept All" on cookie popups ‚Äî choose "Necessary only"

üîí What is a VPN?

VPN = Virtual Private Network

A VPN encrypts your internet connection and routes it through a server in another location. This makes it harder for others to see what you're doing online.

When to use a VPN:

  • On public Wi-Fi (caf√©s, airports, libraries)
  • When accessing sensitive information (banking)
  • When travelling overseas to access Australian content
  • To prevent ISP tracking of browsing habits

‚ö†Ô∏è Never use a free VPN ‚Äî they often sell your data. Paid options: NordVPN, ProtonVPN, Mullvad.

Real Examples

‚ö†Ô∏è Location Stalking

A Perth teenager posted Instagram stories with location tags. A stranger used these to figure out her school, regular routes, and home address. Police intervened after the person showed up at her school.

‚ö†Ô∏è Job Application Rejected

A Year 12 student applied for a scholarship. The selection panel found public Facebook posts with inappropriate content from years earlier. Application rejected.

Privacy is Ongoing

Remember:

  • Apps change their settings and interfaces ‚Äî review every few months
  • New apps = new privacy settings to check
  • Default settings are almost always the LEAST private
  • If a service is free, you're the product (they sell your data)
  • You can't control what others post about you, but you can control what YOU share

Privacy isn't about hiding. It's about control ‚Äî deciding who gets access to your information and when.
