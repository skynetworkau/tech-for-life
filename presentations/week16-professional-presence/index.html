The University Path

What is University?

3-6 year degrees focused on theory, research, and professional careers. You specialise in fields like medicine, engineering, teaching, law, or science.

WA Universities:

  • University of Western Australia (UWA)
  • Curtin University
  • Murdoch University
  • Edith Cowan University (ECU)
  • University of Notre Dame

ATAR & Course Requirements

Your ATAR (Australian Tertiary Admission Rank) is calculated from your Year 12 results. Different courses need different ATARs.

Examples:

  • Medicine: 99+ ATAR
  • Engineering: 80-85 ATAR
  • Arts, Business: 70-75 ATAR
  • Some courses have no ATAR requirement

Don't meet the ATAR? Many unis offer pathway programs, alternative entry, or portfolio entry for creative courses.

TISC ‚Äî Apply to University

What is TISC?

Tertiary Institutions Service Centre ‚Äî the central place to apply for undergraduate uni courses in WA.

How it works:

  • Create a TISC account at tisc.edu.au
  • List up to 6 course preferences in order
  • TISC uses your ATAR to match you to courses
  • You get offers in January (main round)

Paying for Uni ‚Äî HECS-HELP

üí∞

You don't pay upfront

Australian citizens and permanent residents can use HECS-HELP (Higher Education Loan Program). The government pays your fees, and you pay them back later through the tax system.

üìä

You only repay when you earn enough

You start repaying your HECS debt when you earn over approximately $54,000 per year. Repayments are automatic through tax. No interest ‚Äî just indexed to inflation.

üìö

Study Assist ‚Äî studyassist.gov.au

Visit Study Assist to calculate costs, understand HECS-HELP, and check if you're eligible for scholarships or Youth Allowance (Centrelink support for students).

AI at University

ü§ñ

Every university has AI policies now

Using AI tools like ChatGPT, Copilot, or Claude is increasingly common, but universities require transparency. Check your course outline for specific rules.

‚öñÔ∏è

Academic integrity ‚Äî what's allowed

AI can help with research, brainstorming, and editing. But submitting AI-generated text as your own work is plagiarism. Always cite AI if you use it.

üîç

Using AI for research

Tools like NotebookLM can help summarise research papers. Always verify facts and cite your sources. AI can be wrong.

üéì

Turnitin and AI detection

Universities use Turnitin to check for plagiarism and AI-generated content. Don't rely on AI to write your assignments. Use it as a tool, not a replacement for your thinking.

The TAFE Path

What is TAFE?

TAFE (Technical and Further Education) offers practical, hands-on courses from Certificate I to Advanced Diploma. It's focused on real-world skills you can use immediately.

WA TAFE Providers:

  • North Metropolitan TAFE
  • South Metropolitan TAFE
  • Regional TAFEs (Great Southern, etc.)

Course Areas

  • Trades: Electrical, plumbing, carpentry, automotive
  • Business: Accounting, admin, management
  • Creative: Graphic design, film, music
  • Health: Nursing, aged care, childcare
  • Hospitality: Commercial cookery, patisserie
  • IT: Networking, cybersecurity, programming

Can lead to uni later: Many TAFE diplomas offer credit toward university degrees (pathway courses).

Enrolling in TAFE

üÜî

USI ‚Äî Unique Student Identifier

You MUST have a USI to enrol in any VET course (TAFE, apprenticeships, traineeships). It's a free reference number that stays with you for life. Create yours at usi.gov.au.

üí≥

VET Student Loans

For diploma-level courses, you can apply for a VET Student Loan (like HECS-HELP for TAFE). You pay the loan back through the tax system when you earn enough.

üîé

Find TAFE courses at training.gov.au

Search for courses by name, qualification level, or field. Compare different TAFEs and check entry requirements.

The Apprenticeship Path

What is an Apprenticeship?

A full-time training contract where you earn while you learn. You work for an employer and study at TAFE part-time (usually one day per week or in blocks).

Duration: 3-4 years

Outcome: Nationally recognised trade qualification (Certificate III or IV)

Common Trades

  • Electrician ‚Äî residential, commercial, industrial
  • Plumber ‚Äî plumbing, gasfitting, drainage
  • Carpenter ‚Äî building, joinery, cabinet making
  • Automotive ‚Äî mechanics, spray painting
  • Chef ‚Äî commercial cookery, patisserie
  • Bricklayer, Painter, Tiler

Great demand in WA: Tradies are in high demand, especially in regional areas. Job security and good pay.

Finding an Apprenticeship

üá¶üá∫

Australian Apprenticeships

Start at australianapprenticeships.gov.au. Browse trades, find local opportunities, and learn about incentives for apprentices (government payments).

üè¢

Group Training Organisations (GTOs)

GTOs employ apprentices and place them with host companies. They handle your pay, training, and support. Search for "GTO Western Australia" to find local ones.

üìû

Reach out directly to local businesses

Call or visit tradies in Denmark and the Great Southern. Many small businesses prefer local apprentices. Bring a resume and be ready to start.

üíº

Check Seek, Indeed, and job boards

Apprenticeships are advertised like jobs. Search "apprentice electrician WA" or "plumbing apprenticeship Great Southern."

The Traineeship Path

What is a Traineeship?

Similar to an apprenticeship, but usually shorter (1-2 years) and in non-trade fields. You work full-time and study part-time for a Certificate III.

Common fields:

  • Business administration
  • Retail
  • Childcare and education
  • IT support
  • Hospitality

Benefits

  • Earn a wage while getting qualified
  • Shorter time commitment than apprenticeships
  • Often leads to permanent employment
  • Recognised qualification on completion

Great for career changers: Traineeships are also for adults switching careers, not just school leavers.

Direct to Work

üõçÔ∏è

Retail and Hospitality

Entry-level roles in shops, cafes, restaurants, and hotels. These jobs teach customer service, teamwork, and time management. Many people work up to management or start their own businesses.

üìã

Administration and Office Work

Receptionists, data entry, office assistants. These roles build skills in communication, organisation, and software (Microsoft Office, Google Workspace).

üåæ

Agriculture and Regional Work

Farm work, shearing, machinery operation. Great for people who love outdoor, physical work. Pays well, especially in harvest seasons.

üìö

Work and study part-time

Many people combine entry-level work with part-time TAFE or online courses. You build experience, earn money, and gain qualifications at the same time.

Taking a Gap Year

What is a Gap Year?

Taking a year off before starting uni, TAFE, or an apprenticeship. It's a chance to work, travel, volunteer, or just figure out what you want to do.

Common gap year activities:

  • Working full-time and saving money
  • Travelling (Australia or overseas)
  • Volunteering or community work
  • Taking short courses or learning new skills

Deferring University Offers

If you get a uni offer through TISC, you can defer (postpone) your start date for one year. This holds your place while you take a gap year.

Pro tip: Many students come back from a gap year more focused and motivated. It's not "falling behind" ‚Äî it's taking time to grow.

Is a Gap Year Right for You?

If you're unsure what to study, burned out, or want life experience before committing, a gap year can be a great choice.

Pathways Aren't Permanent

üîÑ

You can change your mind

Many people switch courses, change careers, or go back to study later. There's no "one path forever." Life is flexible.

üéì

Mature age entry to university

If you didn't go to uni straight after school, you can apply as a mature age student (usually 21+). Your work experience and TAFE qualifications count toward entry.

üìú

Credit transfer between courses

If you study a TAFE diploma, you might get credit toward a uni degree. If you change uni courses, your subjects might transfer too.

üíº

Most people change careers multiple times

The average person changes careers 5-7 times in their life. Don't feel locked in. Your first choice is just that ‚Äî your first.

Activity ‚Äî Explore Your Pathway

Pathway Exploration Task

  1. Pick ONE pathway that interests you (uni, TAFE, apprenticeship, or work).
  2. Look up ONE specific thing:
    • University: Find a course on TISC and check the ATAR requirement.
    • TAFE: Search for a certificate or diploma on training.gov.au.
    • Apprenticeship: Find a trade on Australian Apprenticeships.
    • Work: Search for entry-level jobs in your area on Seek.
  3. Write down:
    • What you found (course name, job title, etc.)
    • What's the first step to apply?
    • Does this pathway still interest you? Why or why not?

Take 15 minutes. Ask for help if you need it!
