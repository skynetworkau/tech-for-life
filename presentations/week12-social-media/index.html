Real Stories

The Teacher Who Lost Her Job
A newly qualified teacher in Perth posted photos from a friend's 21st birthday party on Instagram. The photos showed her drinking and wearing revealing clothing. Even though the account was "private," a parent screenshotted the images and sent them to the school board. She was dismissed during her probation period.

Another graduate applied for 15 hospitality jobs. She got zero callbacks. When she Googled herself, she found old Facebook posts from Year 10 where she complained about her job at Hungry Jack's and called customers "annoying." She deleted them, waited a month, and got 3 interview offers from her next round of applications.
‚ö† Lesson: "Private" doesn't mean safe. What you post at 16 can affect you at 21 ‚Äî or 25.

Having Nothing Online?

Also a Problem
47% of employers say they're less likely to interview a candidate if they can't find them online.

Why? Because employers want to verify you're a real person, see how you communicate, and get a sense of your personality and interests. A complete absence looks suspicious in 2026.

The solution isn't to hide completely ‚Äî it's to control what's visible.
‚úì Aim for: A professional LinkedIn profile + clean, locked-down personal accounts
