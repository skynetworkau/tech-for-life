Know Your Enemy

ATS: The Resume Robot

What It Is

Software that scans resumes for keywords and phrases before a human sees them.

It's looking for exact matches to the job ad. "Customer service" ‚â† "helping people" to a robot.

The Bad News

Your beautiful creative resume with graphics, columns, and fancy fonts? ATS can't read it. Straight to the shadow realm.

How ATS Kills Resumes

  • Fancy formatting (tables, columns, text boxes)
  • Graphics and images (it's blind to them)
  • Wrong file format (some can't read PDFs)
  • Missing keywords from the job ad
  • Creative job titles instead of standard ones

That internship at your uncle's business? If you called yourself "Brand Ambassador" instead of "Marketing Assistant", the ATS won't find you.

Cheat Code #1

Keyword Hacking

üîë

Copy keywords from the job ad INTO your resume

Literally copy-paste the exact phrases they use. If they say "team player", don't say "works well with others".

ü§ñ

AI is literal, not smart

It's scanning for exact matches. "Microsoft Excel" ‚â† "spreadsheet software". Use their language.

üéØ

Example: Job ad says "customer service"

Bad: "Helped customers and answered questions"
Good: "Provided customer service in fast-paced retail environment"

üîß Tool: Jobscan.co

Paste the job ad + your resume. It shows you exactly what keywords you're missing. Free version gives you enough. Basically cheating, but legal.

Cheat Code #2

ATS-Friendly Format

‚ùå ATS Hates This

  • Two-column layouts
  • Tables and text boxes
  • Headers and footers with info
  • Graphics, logos, icons
  • Fancy fonts (script, decorative)
  • Photos (unless hospitality/retail)

‚úì ATS Loves This

  • Single-column layout
  • Clear section headings (Education, Experience, Skills)
  • Simple fonts (Arial, Calibri, Times)
  • .docx format (unless they ask for PDF)
  • Standard bullet points
  • Plain text formatting

Save the creativity for your portfolio. For job applications, boring wins. Get past the robot first, impress the human later.

Still Works for AI

The STAR Method

AI loves structure. Give it structure.

S ‚Äî Situation

Set the scene. Where were you? What was happening? Keep it brief (2-3 sentences).

T ‚Äî Task

What did you need to do? What was your responsibility or goal?

A ‚Äî Action

What YOU specifically did. Use "I", not "we". This is your moment.

R ‚Äî Result

What happened? Use numbers if possible. "Increased sales by 15%" hits harder than "did well".

üí° Example

Question: "Tell me about a time you solved a problem."

S: "At my retail job, we were understaffed during the Christmas rush."
T: "I needed to keep customers happy despite long wait times."
A: "I reorganised the queue system and communicated wait times upfront."
R: "Customer complaints dropped by half and my manager put me on permanent rostering."
