The Line: OK vs Not OK

‚úì ACCEPTABLE Use

  • Brainstorming and generating ideas
  • Understanding difficult concepts
  • Creating essay outlines and structures
  • Checking grammar and clarity
  • Getting feedback on your work
  • Creating practice questions
  • Summarising research you've read

‚úó UNACCEPTABLE Use

  • Having AI write your entire essay
  • Copying AI text directly as your own
  • Using AI during exams (unless allowed)
  • Having AI do maths problems without learning how
  • Submitting AI-generated code as your own
  • Using AI when told not to by your teacher
  • Not acknowledging AI assistance when required

Academic Integrity

ü§î The Test

Ask yourself:

  • Am I still doing the learning?
  • Could I explain this work to my teacher?
  • Would I be comfortable telling my teacher how I used AI?
  • Am I following my teacher's specific instructions?

If you answered "no" to any of these, you've crossed the line.

üìã When to Acknowledge AI

You should state that you used AI when:

  • It's required by your teacher or assignment brief
  • AI helped generate significant content or ideas
  • You're unsure ‚Äî always better to disclose

Simple format: "I used ChatGPT to help brainstorm ideas and check grammar."

WACE & University Policies

üìö WACE Exams

NO AI tools are permitted in external WACE exams. This includes any device with internet access. Using AI during an exam is considered cheating and will result in severe penalties.

üìù School Assessments

Each teacher sets their own rules for assignments. Always check with your teacher before using AI for any assessment task. When in doubt, ask!

üéì University Level

WA universities (UWA, Curtin, Murdoch, ECU) are developing AI policies. Most expect you to use AI ethically and acknowledge its use. Standards are similar to high school: using AI to learn is encouraged, using it to cheat is not. Always read the unit outline for specific policies.

Policies are evolving. Stay updated and always ask if you're unsure.

Real Consequences

‚ö†Ô∏è

In High School

Academic misconduct can result in zero marks, disciplinary action, and notation on your record. For serious breaches, it may affect university applications.

üéì

At University

Academic misconduct is taken very seriously. Penalties range from failing the unit to suspension or expulsion. It becomes part of your permanent academic record.

üíº

In Your Career

Academic misconduct on your record can prevent you from entering certain professions (teaching, law, medicine) and can be discovered in background checks.

üß†

Most Importantly

Using AI to avoid learning means you won't develop the skills you need. You're only cheating yourself.

AI Limitations

‚ùå

AI Makes Mistakes

AI can "hallucinate" facts, dates, quotes, and sources. Always verify important information.

üìÖ

Knowledge Cutoff

Most AI models have a knowledge cutoff date. They don't know about very recent events or research.

ü§ñ

No Real Understanding

AI doesn't truly understand content like a human does. It can miss nuance and context.

üé®

Lacks Creativity

AI is trained on existing work. It can't have truly original insights or perspectives like you can.

‚öñÔ∏è

Bias in Training

AI reflects biases present in its training data. Be critical of the responses you receive.

Your AI Workflow

1

Start With Your Own Thinking

What do you already know? What do you need to learn? Write down your initial ideas first.

2

Use AI to Explore & Understand

Ask questions, get explanations, brainstorm ideas. Use AI as a study partner.

3

Do Your Own Research

Use AI-suggested sources and do deeper reading. Develop your own understanding.

4

Create Your Own Work

Write in your own words, based on your research and understanding. Make it yours.

5

Use AI to Review & Improve

Get feedback on clarity, grammar, and argument strength. Make final improvements yourself.

Privacy & Data Safety

‚ùå Never Share

  • Personal information (address, phone, email)
  • Other people's personal details
  • Passwords or login details
  • Photos with identifiable people/places
  • Confidential school information
  • Private family matters

‚úì Remember

  • AI conversations may be stored and reviewed
  • Free AI tools use your data to improve their models
  • Be vague with context when possible ("my city" not "Perth")
  • Use school-approved AI tools when available
  • Never use AI for sensitive or confidential information

When in doubt, leave it out. Protect your privacy and others'.
