So... What is AI?

Artificial Intelligence is when computers perform tasks that typically require human intelligence ‚Äî like understanding language, recognising images, making decisions, or learning from experience.

It's not sentient. It's not alive. But it can seem pretty clever.

Example: Photo Recognition

Training Phase

  • Show AI 10,000 photos labelled "dog"
  • Show AI 10,000 photos labelled "cat"
  • AI learns patterns: pointy ears vs floppy, whiskers vs snouts, sizes, shapes
  • Builds a mathematical model of "dog-ness" and "cat-ness"

Prediction Phase

  • You show AI a new photo
  • AI compares it to patterns it learned
  • Calculates probability: 92% dog, 8% cat
  • Returns answer: "It's a dog!"

Modern AI can identify thousands of objects with 95%+ accuracy

What AI Can't Do

AI is NOT

  • Truly creative (it remixes patterns)
  • Conscious or self-aware
  • Capable of genuine empathy
  • Able to truly understand meaning
  • Always accurate (it hallucinates)
  • Unbiased (reflects training data biases)

AI is GOOD at

  • Pattern recognition at scale
  • Processing huge datasets quickly
  • Making predictions from data
  • Automating repetitive tasks
  • Assisting human creativity
  • Analysing text, images, and code

AI is a powerful tool, but humans are still needed for judgment, creativity, and ethics
