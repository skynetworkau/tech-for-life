Starting a Business

Everything Year 12 students need to know to start their own business in Australia — from ABN to GST to marketing

Why Start a Business?

Be Your Own Boss

You make the decisions. Set your own schedule. Choose your clients. Work on projects you care about.

Flexibility

Work around school, uni, or another job. Take time off when you need it. Scale up or down as life changes.

Unlimited Income Potential

No hourly cap. Your earnings grow with your effort. Price your own services. Keep all the profits.

Build Something You're Proud Of

Create a brand. Develop skills. Solve real problems. Build a portfolio that shows what you can do.

Tax Advantages

Claim business expenses. Reduce taxable income. Keep more of what you earn through legal deductions.

Side Hustle While Employed

Test ideas without quitting your job. Build gradually. Transition when you're ready (or keep both!).

What is an ABN?

ABN = Australian Business Number

  • 11-digit unique identifier for your business
  • Required to invoice businesses (they need it for their tax records)
  • Lets you claim GST credits (if GST registered)
  • Makes you look professional and legitimate
  • FREE to apply at abr.gov.au
  • Takes 5 minutes online (instant approval for most)
  • You can get one at ANY age (no minimum age requirement)

You don't NEED an ABN to run a small business, but you'll look more professional and avoid tax withholding issues.

Sole Trader Explained

The simplest business structure

What it means: You ARE the business. There's no legal separation between you and the business entity.

  • Use your personal Tax File Number (TFN) for business tax
  • No setup costs or registration fees (except ABN, which is free)
  • No separate bank account required (but recommended for clarity)
  • Business income goes straight on your personal tax return
  • Full control — you make all decisions

⚠️ Personal liability: You're personally responsible for all business debts. If the business owes money, creditors can come after your personal assets.

Company (Pty Ltd) Explained

A separate legal entity

What it means: The company exists separately from you. It can own assets, enter contracts, and be sued in its own name.

  • Limited liability — your personal assets are protected if the business fails
  • Complex setup: $500+ to register with ASIC
  • Separate tax return (company tax rate: 25% for small businesses)
  • More paperwork: AGMs, director duties, ASIC reporting
  • Better for: large businesses, investors, high-risk ventures

For most side hustles and small businesses, a company is overkill. Start as a sole trader.

GST — Goods & Services Tax

The 10% tax on most goods and services

Key rule: You only need to register for GST if your business earns $75,000+ per year.

  • Below $75k: GST registration is optional (most side hustles don't need it)
  • If registered: Charge 10% GST on invoices, lodge BAS quarterly, claim GST credits on business purchases
  • If NOT registered: Simpler — no BAS to lodge, but can't claim GST credits
  • GST is paid by the customer, you collect and pass it to the ATO

For most Year 12 side hustles earning under $75k, skip GST registration to keep it simple.

Tax as a Sole Trader

How It Works

  • Business income goes on your personal tax return
  • Pay tax at your marginal rate (0% up to $18,200, then 19%, 32.5%, etc.)
  • Claim business expenses to reduce taxable income
  • Tax return due 31 October (or later with accountant)
  • If earning over ~$4,000, may need to pay quarterly PAYG instalments

Golden Rules

  • Keep receipts for EVERYTHING you spend on the business
  • Set aside 25-30% of income for tax (in a separate account)
  • Use accounting software or spreadsheets to track income/expenses
  • Don't mix personal and business money (separate bank account)
  • Consider hiring an accountant for your first tax return

What You Can Claim as Deductions

Equipment & Tools

Laptop, phone, camera, lawn mower, tools — anything used for the business. If also used personally, claim business %.

Phone & Internet

Claim the % used for business. E.g., 50% business use = claim 50% of monthly bill. Keep records of usage.

Vehicle Costs

Fuel, insurance, rego for business travel. Use logbook method or cents per km (78c/km for 2025). Keep records.

Home Office

If you work from home, claim a portion of rent/mortgage, electricity, internet. Use ATO's fixed rate method (67c/hour).

Marketing & Advertising

Website hosting, business cards, Facebook ads, Google ads, signage, flyers — all deductible.

Subscriptions & Software

Xero, Adobe Creative Cloud, Canva Pro, domain names, email hosting, Zoom — claim 100% if business-only.

More Deductions

Other things you can claim

  • Professional development: courses, books, workshops related to your business
  • Bank fees for business accounts
  • Accounting and bookkeeping fees
  • Business insurance
  • Postage and delivery costs
  • Stationery and office supplies
  • Protective clothing / uniforms (with business logo)

⚠️ The golden rule: You can only claim expenses that are directly related to earning business income. Keep all receipts for 5 years.

Setting Up Your Business

1

Apply for ABN (free, 5 minutes)

Go to abr.gov.au → Apply for ABN → Answer questions → Instant approval. You'll need your TFN.

2

Open a Business Bank Account

Separate business from personal money. Makes tax time easier. Most banks offer free business accounts for sole traders.

3

Create an Invoice Template

Use Canva, Word, or accounting software. Include: name, ABN, services, amount, payment terms.

4

Set Up Accounting (Xero, MYOB, or Spreadsheet)

Track income and expenses from day 1. Makes tax time painless. Even a simple spreadsheet works.

5

Get Insurance if Needed

Public liability insurance (~$200-400/year) protects you if you damage property or injure someone.

6

Start Finding Customers!

Word of mouth, social media, local community groups, flyers — just start talking about your business.

Invoicing Basics

What Must Be On An Invoice

  • Your name/business name and ABN
  • Your contact details (email, phone)
  • Customer name and details
  • Invoice date and unique invoice number
  • Description of goods/services provided
  • Amount charged (include GST if registered)
  • Payment terms (e.g., due in 14 days)
  • Payment method (bank details, PayPal, etc.)

Payment & Invoicing Platforms

Don't reinvent the wheel — use systems that handle invoicing, payments, and billing for you

Stripe

Online payments made easy. Accept cards on your website or via payment links.

  • 1.75% + 30c per transaction
  • Recurring billing for subscriptions
  • Professional invoices with payment link
  • Instant transfer to your bank

Square

Best for in-person payments. Tap-and-go card reader for markets, events, mobile services.

  • 1.6% per tap transaction
  • Free card reader to get started
  • Built-in invoicing and appointments
  • Point of sale app on your phone

PayPal Business

Trusted by customers worldwide. Easy invoicing with "Pay Now" button.

  • ~2.6% + 30c per transaction
  • Customers don't need PayPal account
  • Professional invoices in minutes
  • Buyer protection builds trust

Xero

Full accounting with built-in invoicing. Popular in Australia.

  • $15-50/month depending on plan
  • Send invoices, auto-reminders
  • Bank feeds, expense tracking
  • Connects to Stripe/PayPal

MYOB (AU favourite)

Australian accounting software. Invoicing, BAS, payroll — all in one.

  • $15-65/month depending on plan
  • Designed for Australian tax (BAS ready)
  • Invoice templates, payment tracking
  • Mobile app for invoicing on the go

Wave (FREE!)

100% free accounting and invoicing. Perfect for starting out.

  • Free invoicing and accounting
  • Only pay if you accept payments
  • Professional templates
  • Great for side hustles

Pro tip: Start with Wave or Square for free, upgrade to Xero/MYOB as you grow

Why Use Integrated Systems?

Benefits of Payment Platforms

  • Look professional — branded invoices, not Word docs
  • Get paid faster — "Pay Now" button right on invoice
  • Automatic reminders — no awkward "where's my money" texts
  • Track everything — see what's paid, overdue, and pending
  • Accept cards — customers prefer card over bank transfer
  • Recurring billing — set up subscriptions that charge automatically

What's Included

  • Invoice creation with your branding
  • Payment links customers can click
  • Multiple payment methods (card, PayPal, Apple Pay)
  • GST calculations (if registered)
  • Receipt sent automatically
  • Expense tracking for deductions
  • Reports for tax time

Real example: Sarah uses Square for her dog walking business. Clients book online, pay upfront with card, and she gets automatic reminders. She spends 0 hours chasing payments.

Pricing Your Services

How to work out what to charge

  • Research competitors — what do they charge for similar services?
  • Don't undervalue yourself — your time is worth money
  • Factor in: time, materials, travel, skill level, and tax (remember to set aside 25-30%)
  • Start slightly lower to build reputation, then raise prices as you get busy
  • Consider packages vs hourly rates (e.g., $200/month for social media vs $50/hour)
  • Be transparent — no hidden fees, clear pricing upfront

Example: Lawn mowing — $40 for small, $55 for medium, $70 for large. Clear, simple, fair.

Common Mistakes to Avoid

Don't make these errors

  • Not separating personal and business money (makes tax impossible)
  • Not keeping receipts (can't claim deductions without proof)
  • Underpricing services (you'll burn out working for peanuts)
  • Not chasing unpaid invoices (be polite but firm — you deserve to be paid)
  • Forgetting to save for tax (set aside 25-30% of every payment)
  • No written agreements (always confirm scope, price, and deadline in writing)
  • Giving up too early (most businesses take 6-12 months to gain momentum)
