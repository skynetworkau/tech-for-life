How TISC Works

What is TISC?

  • Tertiary Institutions Service Centre
  • You apply through TISC, not directly to unis
  • One application for all WA unis
  • Processes your preferences

The Process

  • Select up to 6 course preferences
  • List them in order of preference
  • Your top available offer is made
  • Apply September-December Year 12
  • Receive offers in January

tisc.edu.au - Applications open early September

ATAR Explained

90.00

ATAR 90 = You're in the top 10% of all Year 12 students

It's a Percentile Rank, Not a Score

ATAR = Australian Tertiary Admission Rank. It shows your position relative to other students. ATAR 75 means you performed better than 75% of the state. It's calculated from your best 4 subjects.

What if My ATAR Isn't Enough?

Pathway Programs
UniReady (Curtin), UWay (UWA), OnTrack (Murdoch) - Complete a bridging course, then enter your degree.
TAFE Articulation
Complete a TAFE diploma, then transfer into 2nd year of uni degree with credit.
Alternative Entry
Portfolio entry (creative courses), interviews, special consideration for hardship or disadvantage.
Try Again Next Year
Gap year, work experience, re-sit exams. Many students defer and reapply with better results.

ATAR is ONE pathway, not the only pathway

HECS-HELP Explained

It's NOT Like Normal Debt

The government pays your fees upfront. You only repay through tax when you earn above $54,435/year. No minimum monthly payments. No credit checks. No interest - just indexed to inflation.

Repayment

1-10% of income above $54,435

Average Debt

$20,000 - $50,000 total

Forgiven If...

You never earn enough or pass away

The Reality Check

30% don't finish
Around 30% of students don't complete their degree. It's OK to change direction.
Changing is common
It's fine to switch courses. Do it early (before 2nd year) to minimize lost time and money.
Deferring is OK
Many students defer for a year to work, travel, or figure out what they want. You can defer your offer.
Research job outcomes
Some degrees have limited job prospects. Check graduate employment rates before committing 3-4 years.

Working While Studying

The Balance

  • Most students work 10-20 hrs/week
  • More than 20 hrs affects grades
  • Campus jobs: library, admin, tutoring
  • Flexible retail/hospitality common

Centrelink Support

  • Youth Allowance (under 25)
  • Austudy (25+)
  • Income and asset tested
  • ~$500-700/fortnight if eligible

servicesaustralia.gov.au/youth-allowance - Apply early, processing takes weeks

Key Takeaways

1
Apply through TISC (tisc.edu.au) in September-December with up to 6 preferences
2
HECS-HELP is not scary debt - you only repay when earning $54,435+
3
ATAR alternatives exist: pathway programs, TAFE, portfolio entry, special consideration
4
Uni is very different to school: less contact time, more self-direction, larger assessments
5
Research career outcomes before committing - not all degrees lead to clear job paths
6
Use support services, apply for scholarships, and it's OK to change your mind
