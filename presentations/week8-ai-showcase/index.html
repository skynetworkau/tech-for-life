Presentation Guidelines

Format

  • Show your AI tool/workflow in action
  • Explain why you chose it
  • Demonstrate the results
  • Share what you learned

What to Show

  • Live demo (preferred)
  • Screenshots or screen recording
  • Before/after comparisons
  • Your creative process
  • Unexpected discoveries

Demo Time Structure

Each Presentation Should Include:

  1. Introduction (30 seconds)
    • What AI tool(s) you used
    • What problem you were solving
  2. Demo (1-2 minutes)
    • Show it in action
    • Highlight key features or techniques
  3. Reflection (30 seconds)
    • What surprised you?
    • What would you do differently?
    • Any ethical considerations?

Peer Feedback Framework

While watching your classmates present, consider:

Positive Feedback

  • What impressed you most?
  • What was creative or innovative?
  • What could you use in your own work?
  • What questions do you have?

Constructive Ideas

  • How could this be expanded?
  • What other tools might work?
  • What ethical considerations arise?
  • How could this be improved?

Keep feedback specific, kind, and actionable
