Trades & Apprenticeships

Everything Year 12 students need to know about TAFE, apprenticeships, and trades careers in WA

Week 13 • Tech for Life • Year 12

Why Consider Trades?

Earn While You Learn

Get paid from day one. No waiting 3-4 years to start earning. You're working and studying at the same time.

No HECS Debt

TAFE is much cheaper than uni. No massive student loans hanging over your head for years.

High Demand = Job Security

Trades are in short supply across WA. You'll always have work. Employers are desperate for skilled tradespeople.

Good Money (Especially in WA Mining)

Qualified tradies earn $70k-$150k+. FIFO mining jobs pay even more. Overtime pushes earnings higher.

Physical, Hands-On Work

If you hate sitting at a desk all day, trades offer variety, movement, and practical problem-solving.

Can Start Your Own Business

Once qualified, you can work for yourself. Set your own rates. Be your own boss. Build something you're proud of.

In-Demand Trades in WA 2026

Electricians

Residential, commercial, industrial work. Essential in construction and mining. High demand across the state.

Plumbers

Domestic and commercial plumbing. Gas fitting. Drainage. Always needed. Great for self-employment.

Carpenters

Building houses, fit-outs, formwork. Construction booming in WA. Transferable skills for renovation work.

Diesel Mechanics / Fitters

Mining machinery, trucks, heavy equipment. Huge demand in mining regions. Excellent FIFO opportunities.

Air Con / Refrigeration

Essential in WA's hot climate. Commercial and residential. Service, installation, maintenance work.

Welders & Bricklayers

Welders: mining, construction, fabrication. Bricklayers: housing boom driving demand. Both highly paid.

How Apprenticeships Work

1

Find an Employer Who'll Take You On

Apply to companies directly, or go through a Group Training Organisation (GTO). They're actively looking for apprentices.

2

Sign a Training Contract

Legal agreement between you, employer, and TAFE. Outlines pay, conditions, training requirements.

3

Work 4 Days, Study 1 Day (Often Block Release)

Spend most time on the job learning practical skills. Attend TAFE for theory (either weekly or in blocks).

4

TAFE for Theory, Workplace for Practical

TAFE teaches principles, calculations, regulations. Work teaches real-world application and skills.

5

Get Paid the Whole Time

Earn a wage from day one. Increases each year. Paid even when attending TAFE.

Finding an Apprenticeship

Group Training Organisations (GTOs)

They employ you as an apprentice, then host you out to companies. Handle support and paperwork.

Apply Directly to Companies

Email your resume to local builders, electricians, plumbers. Many advertise on their websites.

SEEK, Indeed, Gumtree

Job boards have dedicated apprenticeship sections. Set up alerts for trades you're interested in.

Word of Mouth

Ask family, friends, teachers. Someone always knows a tradesperson looking for an apprentice.

Career Expos

Attend local career expos. Speak to employers face-to-face. Make a good impression.

Try Before: Work Experience

Do work experience in Year 11-12. Shows commitment. Employer might take you on after school.

Group Training Organisations

What are GTOs and why use them?

GTOs employ you as an apprentice and then place you with host companies for on-the-job training. They handle the admin and provide support throughout your apprenticeship.

  • They employ you directly (you're their employee)
  • Host you out to different companies for work experience
  • Handle all paperwork, contracts, and TAFE enrolment
  • Provide support if you have issues with a host employer
  • Good for variety: work with multiple companies in different settings
  • Backup safety net: if host company closes, GTO finds you another placement

Examples in WA: MEGT, Apprenticeships WA, Group Training SW, WAGIN, MAS National

TAFE in WA

TAFE Colleges in WA

  • North Metropolitan TAFE (Perth northern suburbs)
  • South Metropolitan TAFE (Perth southern suburbs)
  • Central Regional TAFE (Geraldton, Kalgoorlie)
  • South Regional TAFE (Bunbury, Albany, Esperance)

What TAFE Offers

  • Certificates I-IV (trade qualifications)
  • Diplomas and Advanced Diplomas
  • Apprenticeship and traineeship training
  • Can articulate into university (pathway option)
  • Much cheaper than uni (often free for apprentices)

TAFE WA is the largest training provider in the state

RTOs: Alternatives to TAFE

What are RTOs?

Registered Training Organisations are private training providers that can deliver nationally recognised qualifications - just like TAFE.

  • Same qualifications as TAFE (Certificate III, IV, etc.)
  • Often more flexible scheduling
  • Sometimes faster completion
  • Smaller class sizes
  • Industry-focused training
  • Can be better for some trades

WA RTOs for Trades

  • SMYL Community College — construction, business
  • MPA Skills — plumbing, gas fitting
  • PEER — electrical, instrumentation
  • AITEC — air con, refrigeration
  • National Training Alliance — automotive
  • Master Painters WA — painting trades

Pro Tip: Your employer often chooses the training provider. Some industries have preferred RTOs.

TAFE isn't your only option — private RTOs can be just as good, sometimes better for specific trades

What You'll Need

White Card

Construction safety induction. Required for all construction sites. ~$70, one-day course. Get it before applying.

Driver's Licence

Highly recommended (almost essential). You'll need to get to job sites, pick up materials, drive work vehicles.

Own Tools

Many trades require you to have basic tools. Employer often helps with tool allowance or provides initially.

Steel Cap Boots

Safety footwear mandatory on most sites. Invest in good quality boots ($100-200). Your feet will thank you.

Appropriate PPE

Hi-vis clothing, hard hat, safety glasses, gloves. Some provided by employer, some you'll buy yourself.

Physical Fitness

Trades are physically demanding. Lifting, climbing, standing all day. Build strength and endurance.

Career Progression

1

Apprentice (3-4 years)

Learning the trade. Getting paid while gaining skills. Building experience and confidence.

2

Qualified Tradesperson

Fully licensed. Working independently. Good hourly rate. Can work anywhere in Australia.

3

Leading Hand

Supervising small teams. Higher pay. More responsibility. Coordinating work on site.

4

Foreman / Supervisor

Managing larger projects. Running job sites. Coordinating trades. Significant pay increase.

5

Start Your Own Business / Specialise

Work for yourself. Build your own team. Or: add specialisations, become a trainer, move into management.

Mining Industry

FIFO Lifestyle

  • Fly In Fly Out — live anywhere, work up north
  • Typical roster: 2 weeks on, 1 week off (varies)
  • Flights and accommodation paid for
  • Long hours but great pay
  • Time away from family and friends
  • Not for everyone — lifestyle trade-offs

The Money

  • Very high wages: $100k-$150k+ common
  • Overtime rates push earnings even higher
  • Entry trades: diesel fitter, electrician, boilermaker
  • Experienced tradies can earn $200k+
  • Save quickly for house deposit, car, travel
  • Tax-effective if you plan it right

WA mining is booming. Huge demand for trades across Pilbara and Goldfields.

Pre-Apprenticeship Programs

Taste the trade before committing

Pre-apprenticeship programs are short Certificate II courses that introduce you to a trade. They help you figure out if it's right for you AND make you more attractive to employers.

  • Usually Certificate II level (entry qualification)
  • Taste of the trade before signing 3-4 year contract
  • Significantly improves your chances of getting an apprenticeship
  • Shows commitment and genuine interest to employers
  • Available at TAFE and private training providers
  • Some available in Year 11-12 through VET programs at school

Ask your school careers counsellor about VET (Vocational Education and Training) options.

Common Questions

Can I change trades?

Yes, but you may lose time and have to start over. Try before you commit through work experience.

What if employer goes bust?

If you're with a GTO, they find you a new host. If employed directly, you may need to find a new employer.

Can I go to uni later?

Absolutely. Many trade qualifications articulate into uni degrees. Pathways exist in engineering, construction management, etc.

Is it too physical?

Depends on the trade. Some are very physical (bricklaying), others less so (air con, electrical). Choose what suits you.

What about women in trades?

Growing demand and support. Many programs encourage women. Same pay, same opportunities. Diversity valued.

Do I need good grades?

Not necessarily. Employers value attitude, reliability, and willingness to learn over ATAR scores.
