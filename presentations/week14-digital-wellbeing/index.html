Why This Matters

In 6 months, you'll be applying for jobs, uni, or apprenticeships. You'll need to lodge tax returns, maybe apply for Youth Allowance, and manage your own healthcare.

Right now, you have teachers, parents, and school support. Use it.

Tax File Number (TFN)

Australian Taxation Office

Why you need one: Your employer MUST have your TFN to pay you correctly. Without it, you'll be taxed at the highest rate (47%) and won't get a refund.

How to apply: Go to ato.gov.au and search "Apply for a TFN". You'll need ID (birth certificate, passport, or licence) and proof of identity.

Takes 28 days to arrive by mail. Apply NOW if you don't have one. Don't wait until you get a job offer.

Once you have it, keep it secure. You'll use the same TFN your whole life.

Bank Account Setup

Getting Paid Needs an Account in Your Name

If you don't have one: Most banks offer youth accounts with no monthly fees for under 25s. You can open one online with ID.

Set up online banking: Download your bank's app. Enable notifications. Learn to check your balance.

PayID is essential: This lets employers pay you using just your mobile number or email. Set this up through your banking app. Makes getting paid instant.

Compare accounts: Look for no monthly fees, no minimum balance, free ATM withdrawals. Most youth accounts offer this.

Ask about student accounts at your bank. They usually have better features and no fees until you're 25.

Centrelink & Youth Allowance

Centrelink

Government financial support for students, job seekers, and apprentices

Who's Eligible?

  • Full-time uni or TAFE students (under 25)
  • Australian Apprentices
  • Job seekers (16-21 years old)
  • Depends on your income and parents' income

How to Apply

Apply online via myGov ‚Üí Centrelink. You'll need your TFN, bank details, and proof of study/work/job search.

If you're going to uni in 2027, apply for Youth Allowance in January. Processing takes 4-6 weeks.

Create a professional email NOW: firstname.lastname@gmail.com or similar. No nicknames, no numbers unless necessary.

Set up an email signature: Your full name, mobile number, and LinkedIn profile (if you have one). Makes you look professional.

Use this email for job applications, myGov, bank accounts, and anything official. Keep your personal/social email separate.

Your Own Phone Plan

Getting Off Your Parents' Plan

Why now? When you move out or start working full-time, you'll need your own plan. Setting it up now means one less thing to worry about later.

What you actually need: Most people use way less data than they think. 10-20GB is usually enough if you have wifi at home/school/work.

Compare plans: Use comparison sites like Whistleout.com.au or Finder.com.au to see what's available. Prepaid plans are good for students ‚Äî no bill shock.

Budget options: Aldi Mobile, Boost, Belong, and Amaysim offer cheap plans on major networks. $15-30/month for plenty of data.

üí°
Don't lock into long contracts. Month-to-month plans give you flexibility and are usually better value.
