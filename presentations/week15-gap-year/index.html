Gap Year & Alternatives

All the options for Year 12 students who don't want to go straight to uni or TAFE

Week 15 ‚Ä¢ Tech for Life ‚Ä¢ Year 12

What is a Gap Year?

Taking a year (or more) before further study

  • Working, travelling, volunteering
  • NOT wasting time ‚Äî building experiences
  • Very common in Australia (30-40% of Year 12s take one)
  • Can defer uni offer for 1-2 years
  • Time to figure out what you actually want
  • Arrive at uni more mature and motivated

A gap year is normal. You're not falling behind ‚Äî you're getting ahead in life experience.

Why Take a Gap Year?

Not Sure What to Study

Don't rush into a degree you'll regret. Use the year to explore interests and careers.

Burnt Out from School

13 years of study is exhausting. Take a break before diving into more study.

Want to Save Money First

Work full-time, save for uni, reduce future debt. Start uni with savings instead of stress.

Travel While Young

No mortgage, no kids, no major commitments. This is the easiest time to travel.

Gain Work Experience

Build your resume, learn workplace skills, get references for future applications.

Figure Out What You Want

Real-world experience helps you discover what you actually enjoy doing.

Working First

Why Work Full-Time?

  • Save money fast (no uni fees yet)
  • Learn workplace skills and professionalism
  • Build references and resume
  • Figure out what you DON'T want to do
  • Gain independence and confidence

Common Gap Year Jobs

  • Retail (Coles, Woolies, clothing stores)
  • Hospitality (cafes, restaurants, bars)
  • Admin / reception
  • Warehouse / logistics
  • Call centres
  • Trades assistant (paid to learn)

Living at home and working full-time, you could save $20k-30k+ in a year

Travelling

Working Holiday Visa

417/462 visa for other countries. Work + travel in Canada, UK, Japan, etc. Must be 18-30.

Australia Road Trip

Australia is massive. Van life, coastal drive, Uluru. Cheaper than flying overseas.

Southeast Asia

Thailand, Vietnam, Bali. Cheap and close. $2k-3k per month budget (accommodation + food + transport).

Europe

Expensive but amazing. $4k-5k per month. Interrail pass for trains. Hostels keep costs down.

Travel Alone or With Friends?

Solo: more flexible, meet new people. Friends: shared costs, comfort. Both are valid.

Work While Travelling

Hostel work, farm work (WWOOFing), remote freelancing. Extends travel time.

Volunteering

Give back and build your resume

  • Conservation Volunteers Australia ‚Äî environmental projects
  • Wildlife rescue (RSPCA, wildlife centres)
  • Community organisations (youth centres, charities)
  • International volunteering (WWOOFing ‚Äî work on farms for accommodation)
  • Looks great on resume and uni applications
  • Find purpose and meaning while figuring out your path

Volunteering shows initiative, empathy, and commitment ‚Äî all things unis and employers value.

Defence Force Pathways

Gap Year Program

1 year paid contract. Explore careers in Army, Navy, Air Force. No commitment after. Get paid to try it.

ADFA

Australian Defence Force Academy. Degree + career. Paid while studying. Full salary after graduation.

Direct Entry Roles

Technical roles, logistics, admin. No degree needed. Training provided. Travel opportunities.

Benefits

Good pay, travel, training, career progression, discipline, fitness, camaraderie.

Considerations

Commitment required (years, not months). Physical fitness needed. Deployment possible.

How to Apply

defencejobs.gov.au ‚Äî aptitude testing, fitness test, interviews. Process takes months.

WA Police

Become a police officer straight out of school

  • Must be 18+ (can apply before you turn 18)
  • No degree required (police academy training provided)
  • Paid training (28 weeks at WA Police Academy)
  • Starting salary around $80k+ (increases with rank and years)
  • Career progression: constable ‚Üí senior constable ‚Üí sergeant ‚Üí inspector
  • Varied work: traffic, detectives, forensics, community, cybercrime

Challenging but rewarding. Make a real difference in your community. Job security and benefits.

Apply at: police.wa.gov.au/Careers

Mining & Resources

Entry-Level Roles

  • Kitchen hand / catering
  • Cleaner / housekeeping
  • Trades assistant (no experience needed)
  • Operations support
  • Admin / data entry

The Reality

  • FIFO lifestyle (Fly-In Fly-Out, away from home)
  • Very high wages ($80k-$120k+ entry-level)
  • Can save a lot quickly
  • Not for everyone (isolated, tough work)
  • Common rosters: 2 weeks on, 1 week off

WA-specific opportunity ‚Äî mining is huge here. Good way to save fast for future goals.

Other Career Paths

Real Estate Agent

No degree required. Get Certificate IV in Real Estate. Commission-based. High earning potential.

Insurance / Banking

Entry-level roles available. Training provided. Stable career path. Good pay progression.

Travel Agent

Certificate course. Work with bookings, itineraries, clients. Travel perks and discounts.

Personal Trainer

Certificate III & IV in Fitness. Work in gyms or start your own business. Flexible hours.

Beautician / Hairdresser

Apprenticeship or cert course. Build a client base. Can run your own salon eventually.

Photographer / Videographer

Self-taught or short course. Build portfolio. Weddings, events, real estate, social media.

Deferring Uni

Have your cake and eat it too

  • Accept your uni offer through TISC/UAC
  • Then immediately apply to defer for 1-2 years
  • Your place is held (no need to reapply)
  • Work, travel, save money in the meantime
  • Start uni more mature, motivated, and financially prepared
  • Check deferral conditions (some courses have restrictions)

Best of both worlds: guaranteed uni place + gap year experience.

Most courses allow deferral. Medicine, Law, and some competitive courses may not.

Career Flexibility

Your career won't be like your parents' generation

  • You'll have 12+ jobs in your lifetime
  • 5 different careers on average
  • Unlike parents: one job for life is GONE
  • Skills transfer between industries
  • It's OK not to have it all figured out at 18
  • Career pivots are normal, not failures

The pressure to choose the "right" path at 18 is outdated. Your path will evolve as you do.

Portfolio Careers

What is a Portfolio Career?

  • Multiple income streams
  • Day job + side hustle
  • Freelancing + part-time work
  • Build skills in different areas
  • More security through diversity

Examples

  • Barista + photographer on weekends
  • Retail worker + online tutoring
  • Gym trainer + social media manager
  • Admin job + freelance writing
  • Trades work + property investing

Remote work opportunities make portfolio careers easier than ever

Saving Strategies

Smart Saving

  • Live at home (save on rent if possible)
  • Avoid lifestyle creep (don't spend just because you earn)
  • Set a clear savings goal
  • High-interest savings account (Ubank, ING, etc.)
  • Automate savings (transfer on payday)

The Gig Economy

Delivery Services

Uber Eats, Menulog, DoorDash. Flexible hours. Use your own vehicle. $15-25/hour (varies).

Rideshare

Uber, Ola. Need car + full licence. Flexible income. Customer service skills essential.

Task Platforms

Airtasker. Handyman tasks, moving help, cleaning. Set your own rates. Build reputation.

Freelancing

Upwork, Fiverr. Design, writing, coding, admin. Global clients. Build portfolio over time.

The Pros

Flexible hours. Be your own boss. Work when you want. Good for side income.

The Cons

No job security. No sick leave or super (unless you're ABN registered). Income varies.
