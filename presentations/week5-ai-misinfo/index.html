What Are Deepfakes?

The Technology

  • AI-generated media that swaps faces, mimics voices, or creates entirely fake people
  • Machine learning analyses thousands of images/audio samples
  • Neural networks recreate realistic facial movements and speech patterns
  • Accessible tools mean anyone can create convincing fakes in minutes

How They're Made

  • Face-swapping apps like Reface, FaceSwap, DeepFaceLab
  • Voice cloning with ElevenLabs, Descript, Play.ht
  • AI video generators like Synthesia, D-ID, HeyGen
  • Free online tools with no technical knowledge required

Many deepfake tools are marketed as "fun" or "creative" but can be weaponised for misinformation.

AI-Generated Text & Fake News

The Problem

  • ChatGPT & similar tools can generate convincing fake articles in seconds
  • Bot networks flood social media with AI-written propaganda
  • Fake reviews, testimonials, and "news" sites
  • Can mimic specific writing styles and outlets

Australian Examples

  • Fake ABC News articles about major events
  • AI-generated local news sites pushing scams
  • Fabricated quotes from politicians and officials
  • Bot networks spreading misinformation during bushfire season

Over 50% of all online content may be AI-generated by 2026 ‚Äî making verification critical.

What to Do When You Encounter Misinformation

1

Stop & Verify

Don't share immediately. Check the source, search for fact-checks, and look for verification from trusted outlets.

2

Report It

Use platform reporting tools. Flag misinformation on Facebook, Instagram, TikTok, X, and YouTube. Report scams to Scamwatch.

3

Warn Others

Comment on false posts with corrections and fact-check links. Message friends who've shared it. Break the chain.

4

Don't Engage with Rage

Algorithms reward engagement. Arguing or angry replies actually boost misinformation's reach. Report and move on.

Build Your Media Literacy

Critical Questions

  • Who created this? What's their agenda?
  • When was it created? Is it current or recycled?
  • What's the original source? Can I verify it?
  • Who benefits if I believe or share this?
  • Does this trigger a strong emotional response? Why?

Good Habits

  • Read past the headline before forming opinions
  • Check date stamps and context before sharing
  • Follow trusted news sources and fact-checkers
  • Be sceptical of "too good to be true" content
  • Pause before clicking "share"

Media literacy is the 21st-century superpower. Question everything, verify before you share.
