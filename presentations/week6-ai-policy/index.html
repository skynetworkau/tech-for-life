What Employers Expect

üîí

Confidentiality First

Never input company secrets, client data, or personal information into public AI tools. Many companies provide approved, secure AI systems for sensitive work.

‚úì

Verify Everything

AI can hallucinate facts and make confident mistakes. Employers expect you to check AI output before using it, especially in professional documents or communications.

üìù

Disclosure and Transparency

Most workplaces require you to document when and how AI was used, especially for client-facing work, legal documents, or decision-making.

üéØ

Human Accountability

You're responsible for the work you submit, even if AI helped create it. "The AI made a mistake" isn't a valid excuse.

When to Use AI

‚úì Good Use Cases
  • Brainstorming and idea generation
  • Explaining complex concepts you're learning
  • Getting first drafts or outlines
  • Checking grammar and improving clarity
  • Researching multiple perspectives quickly
  • Debugging code or finding errors
  • Creating practice questions for study
  • Summarising long documents
‚úó When to Avoid AI
  • Assessment explicitly prohibits it
  • Work involves personal information
  • You need to demonstrate YOUR learning
  • Medical, legal, or safety-critical decisions
  • When you can't verify the information
  • Creating content you don't understand
  • Situations requiring genuine human empathy
  • When using company/client confidential data

Disclosure and Transparency

üéì

For University Assignments

"AI tools (ChatGPT, Claude) were used to brainstorm initial ideas and check grammar. All final content is my own work and understanding."

üíº

For Work Documents

Check your workplace policy. Many require a note like: "Draft assistance provided by [AI tool]. Content reviewed and verified by [Your Name]."

üé®

For Creative Work

If submitting portfolios or competitions: "This image was created using AI tools (Midjourney/DALL-E) with prompts and curation by [Your Name]."

üí¨

When to Always Disclose

Anytime someone might reasonably assume the work is entirely your own creation or when assessment/workplace policies require it. When in doubt, disclose.

Australian AI Ethics Framework

1
Human-Centred Values
AI systems should respect human rights, diversity, and individual autonomy. People, not technology, remain in control.
2
Fairness
AI must not create unfair bias or discrimination. Systems should be inclusive and accessible to all Australians.
3
Privacy & Security
Personal information must be protected. AI systems should have strong data governance and cybersecurity.
4
Reliability & Safety
AI should operate safely and as intended. Systems must be tested and monitored for risks and failures.
5
Transparency
People should know when they're interacting with AI and understand how decisions affecting them are made.
6
Contestability
People can challenge AI decisions that affect them. There must be human oversight and accountability.
