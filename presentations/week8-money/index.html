The difference? Government takes their share (tax) before you see it!

PAYG Tax (Pay As You Go)

Why Does the Government Take Money From Your Pay?

  • Funds schools, hospitals, roads, and public services
  • Your employer is legally required to withhold tax
  • Prevents you owing a huge tax bill at the end of the year
  • Based on your income and tax bracket
Important: You MUST give your employer a Tax File Number (TFN) or they'll withhold the maximum tax rate (around 47%)!

Get your TFN: Apply online at ato.gov.au when you turn 16
Took around 10-28 days to receive

Superannuation (Super)

11.5%

Your employer MUST pay 11.5% of your gross wage into a super fund
This is ON TOP of your wage, not taken from it!

What is Super?

  • Retirement savings account
  • Locked until you're 60 (usually)
  • Invested and grows over time
  • Compulsory for anyone earning $450+ per month

Example

You earn: $1,000
Super (11.5%): $115
Boss pays you: $1,000
Boss pays super fund: $115
If your employer offers "salary sacrifice to super", that IS taken from your wage - different to compulsory super!

Tax Returns - Get Your Money Back!

31 October

Deadline to lodge your tax return (for 1 July - 30 June financial year)

Why Lodge a Return?

  • Get refunded if you paid too much tax
  • Claim deductions (work expenses)
  • Legal requirement if you earned money
  • Most students GET MONEY BACK

How to Lodge - myTax (Free!)

  • Login to myGov ‚Üí ATO
  • Click "Lodgements" ‚Üí "Tax Return"
  • Info is pre-filled from employers
  • Add deductions (if any)
  • Submit - refund in 2 weeks!
Don't pay for tax agents if you earn under $45,000 and have simple tax. myTax is FREE and easy!

Tax Deductions - What Can You Claim?

Deductions reduce your taxable income = pay less tax (or get bigger refund)

‚úì You CAN Claim

  • Union fees

  • Tools and equipment for work

‚úó You CANNOT Claim

Keep receipts! Scan/photo them and store in a folder. You need proof if the ATO asks. Items under $300 can be claimed in full that year.

Common Money Mistakes to Avoid

1. Multiple Super Accounts

Each job might create a new super fund. Multiple accounts = multiple fees eating your money!
Fix: Consolidate (combine) them via myGov ‚Üí ATO ‚Üí Super

2. Not Lodging Tax Returns

"I didn't earn much, so I don't need to lodge" = WRONG!
Fix: Lodge every year even if you earned $1. Get your refund + avoid ATO penalties.

3. Not Checking Payslips

Employers make mistakes. Underpayment is common!
Fix: Check every payslip matches hours worked and correct pay rates.

4. Ignoring Super Until Retirement

Super grows with compound interest. Start early = retire rich!
Fix: Check quarterly that super is being paid. Choose a good fund with low fees.
